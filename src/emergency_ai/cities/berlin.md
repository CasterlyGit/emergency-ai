## Local laws relevant in emergencies

- **Duty to Render Aid** (Strafgesetzbuch (StGB) §323c): German law imposes a legal duty on every person to render first aid in an emergency if it is necessary and can be expected of them given the circumstances. Failure is a criminal offence (unterlassene Hilfeleistung) punishable by fine or up to 1 year imprisonment.
- **Civil Liability Protection for First Aiders** (BGB §680 + Rettungsgesetz Berlin): A person who renders emergency assistance acts in a state of emergency (Notstand) and is protected from civil liability for ordinary negligence. Only gross negligence or intent can create liability.

## Practical notes

- Germany uses 112 for fire/medical and 110 for police. Both work from any phone including mobiles without SIM.
- Berliner Feuerwehr (fire department) handles all medical emergencies and dispatches paramedics (Rettungssanitäter) or emergency physicians (Notarzt) based on triage.
- U-Bahn/S-Bahn emergency: use the intercom in each car or the red emergency call buttons on platforms. Do not pull the emergency brake between stations.
- Poison Control Berlin (030-19240) is a 24/7 specialist line for toxicological advice.
- German law mandates first aid training for driver's licenses — most adults have basic CPR training.
- Heat warnings (Hitzewarnung) issued by DWD (German Weather Service) at high risk periods; elderly in Gründerzeit buildings without AC are most vulnerable.
