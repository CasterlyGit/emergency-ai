## Emergency numbers

- Police / Fire / Ambulance: **999** (or 112 — works in UK)
- Non-emergency police: **101**
- NHS non-emergency medical: **111**
- Samaritans (mental health, 24/7): **116 123**
- Domestic abuse helpline: **0808 2000 247**

## Local laws relevant in emergencies

- **Duty to act — UK common law:** No general legal duty for bystanders to render aid (unlike some European jurisdictions). However, once aid begins, the giver owes a duty to perform with reasonable care. The **Social Action, Responsibility and Heroism Act 2015** instructs courts to take into account that the person was acting heroically when assessing negligence.
- **Drugs and 999:** No formal Good Samaritan drug-law immunity in England/Wales. However, police guidance generally prioritizes saving life over arrest for personal possession at an overdose scene — but this is discretion, not a statute.
- **Mental health crisis:** Section 136 of the Mental Health Act allows police to detain a person in a public place who appears to need urgent mental health care. The detainee is taken to a "place of safety" (usually a hospital, not a cell) for assessment.
- **Knife crime:** If you find a knife/weapon, **do not pick it up**. Cover it with something inverted (a bin) and call 999 — UK forensics matters.

## Practical notes

- 999 operators ask which service first (police/fire/ambulance). Be ready to choose.
- Ambulances: NHS targets are 7 min for category 1 (life-threatening), 18 min for category 2 (emergency). Outside central London, response can be slower.
- Hospitals: major trauma centers are Royal London (Whitechapel), King's College, St George's, St Mary's, North Middlesex.
- TfL (Tube/bus): in a Tube emergency, use the passenger emergency alarm. Driver will hold at the next station so emergency services can board easily — better than stopping mid-tunnel.
- Fire safety: UK building codes since Grenfell are strict; if you're in a tower block fire, "stay put" advice has changed — follow building's posted plan, but if smoke enters your flat, leave.
