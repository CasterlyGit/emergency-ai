## Emergency numbers

- Primary (police / fire / ambulance): **000**
- Alternate Emergency: **112**
- Police Non Emergency: **131 444**
- Poisons Information: **13 11 26**
- Mental Health Crisis: **1800 011 511**
- Lifeline: **13 11 14**
- Ses State Emergency: **132 500**

## Local laws relevant in emergencies

- **Good Samaritan Act** (NSW Good Samaritan Act 2001): A Good Samaritan who acts in good faith to assist a person apparently in need of emergency assistance is not personally liable for anything done or omitted to be done in the course of providing that assistance, unless the Good Samaritan was impaired by alcohol or drugs.
- **Drug Misuse and Trafficking Act — Limited Immunity** (Drug Misuse and Trafficking Act 1985 (NSW) + 2019 Amendment): NSW introduced drug checking and fixed-site pill testing; limited immunity exists for personal-use quantities at approved events. For overdose 000 calls: police attendance is generally discretionary, and saving a life is the priority.

## Practical notes

- Australia's primary emergency number is 000 (Triple Zero). 112 also works from mobile phones, including without SIM card.
- Ambulance in NSW is fee-for-service unless you have an Ambulance cover (e.g., NRMA or private health). In a life-threatening emergency, always call 000 regardless — billing is secondary.
- Bushfire: monitor NSW RFS (rfs.nsw.gov.au) and Fires Near Me app. If in an evacuation zone, leave early — do not shelter in place against an ember attack without a prepared structure.
- Shark/marine: call 000. Lifeguard towers operate during patrolled hours; always swim between the red and yellow flags.
- Heatwave: Sydney heat is intensifying. Misting stations at beaches; community cooling centres during Code Red heat events.
- Snake bite: apply pressure immobilisation bandage (NOT a tourniquet), immobilise, call 000. NSW has death adders, eastern browns, tiger snakes — all potentially fatal.
