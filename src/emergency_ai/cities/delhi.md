## Local laws relevant in emergencies

- **Good Samaritan Law** (Supreme Court Guidelines 2016 + MoRTH 2020): Anyone who helps a road accident victim is protected from civil/criminal liability and cannot be detained or compelled to disclose identity beyond a voluntary statement.
- **Hospital Emergency Duty** (Pt. Parmanand Katara v. Union of India, 1989): Every doctor, government or private, has a constitutional duty to provide immediate emergency care regardless of medico-legal formalities or payment status.
- **Mental Healthcare Act** (Mental Healthcare Act 2017): Attempted suicide is decriminalized. The person must be provided care and support, not treated as a criminal.

## Practical notes

- 112 is the single unified emergency number covering police, fire, and ambulance across Delhi.
- CATS (Centralized Accident and Trauma Services) ambulances are free, GPS-tracked, and Delhi-specific. Dial 102 or 112.
- Delhi Metro emergencies: press the emergency button inside coaches. CISF security staff are at every station.
- Air quality (AQI): Delhi frequently exceeds 300 AQI in winter (Nov-Jan). At AQI >300, outdoor exertion can trigger respiratory and cardiac events — stay indoors with N95.
- Traffic congestion is severe during peak hours and monsoon. For time-critical emergencies (stroke, MI), consider private vehicle directly to hospital if ambulance ETA is long.
- Heat wave (Apr-Jun): Delhi sees extreme heat above 45°C. Cool the person rapidly — shade, wet cloth, fan — and call 112 immediately.
