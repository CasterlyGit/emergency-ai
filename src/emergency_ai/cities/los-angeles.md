## Local laws relevant in emergencies

- **California Good Samaritan Law** (Health & Safety Code §1799.102): Provides immunity for both medical and non-medical care rendered in good faith at the scene of an emergency. Applies statewide.
- **California 911 Good Samaritan Drug Law** (Health & Safety Code §11376.5): Calling 911 for a drug overdose grants limited immunity for possession and being under the influence. Caller and victim are protected if they remain at the scene and cooperate with emergency services.

## Practical notes

- LAFD and LAPD respond to 911; specify whether you need fire/medical or police to speed routing.
- Wildfire risk is year-round but peaks October-February (Santa Ana winds). If under evacuation order, leave immediately — do not wait.
- Earthquake preparedness: ShakeAlert early warning system provides seconds of warning via Wireless Emergency Alerts. Drop-Cover-Hold-On.
- Freeway accidents: stay in your vehicle if it is drivable and move to the right shoulder. Call 511 or 911 if injuries or debris block lanes.
- Los Angeles County Department of Mental Health crisis line (800-854-7771) dispatches clinicians, not police, for mental health calls.
- Narcan (naloxone) is available without prescription at most LA pharmacies under a statewide standing order.
