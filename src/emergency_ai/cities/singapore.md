## Local laws relevant in emergencies

- **Good Samaritan Provision** (Emergency Medical Services Act (Cap. 85A) + Penal Code §88): Under Penal Code §88, an act done in good faith for the benefit of a person even without consent is not an offence if it is done with due care and attention. SCDF and MOH guidance explicitly encourages bystander CPR; civil suits against good-faith rescuers are extremely rare.

## Practical notes

- Singapore has two emergency numbers: 995 (SCDF — fire and ambulance) and 999 (Singapore Police Force). Use 995 for medical and fire, 999 for police and crime.
- SCDF response time target is under 11 minutes. Singapore is small enough that coverage is generally excellent.
- AEDs are mandated in all MRT stations, community clubs, schools, and commercial buildings. The MyResponder app (SCDF) alerts nearby CPR-trained volunteers when a cardiac arrest is reported.
- MRT emergency: press the intercom or alarm handle. SMRT staff respond very quickly; every station has a first-aid room.
- Singapore Poison Control Centre (6423-9119) at Singapore General Hospital — 24/7 toxicology advice.
- Dengue fever is endemic. High fever, severe headache, eye pain, rash — seek medical attention within 24 hours. Monitor platelet count.
- Strict laws on controlled substances: no Good Samaritan drug immunity statute. Always call 995 for an overdose — saving a life is prioritized.
