## Emergency numbers

- All-emergency single number: **112**
- Police: **100** / Bangalore traffic: **103**
- Fire: **101**
- Ambulance (Karnataka): **108** (Arogya Kavacha, integrated)
- Women's Helpline: **1091**
- Senior Citizen Helpline (BBMP): **1090**
- BBMP Disaster Management: **1077** / **080-22221188**

## Local laws relevant in emergencies

- **Good Samaritan Law (India, Supreme Court 2016 + MoRTH 2020 Guidelines, Karnataka Notification 2018):** Karnataka has explicitly adopted the Good Samaritan guidelines. Anyone aiding a road accident victim cannot be harassed, detained, or compelled to disclose identity. Hospitals must provide immediate care without insisting on police/financial formalities first.
- **Hospital duty (Pt. Parmanand Katara, 1989):** All hospitals, including private, must render immediate emergency care.
- **Karnataka Police Act + Domestic Violence Act 2005:** Protection officers reachable via 1091. Vanitha Sahayavani is a women's police helpdesk in Bangalore.
- **Mental Healthcare Act 2017:** Decriminalized attempted suicide; care, not custody.
- **Cyber-emergencies (financial fraud in progress):** Karnataka has dedicated cyber crime helpline **1930** (national) for immediate freeze of fraudulent transactions; speed matters — first 24 hours are critical.

## Practical notes

- **108 ambulance** is GPS-dispatched and free in Karnataka. Coverage in city is generally < 15-20 min during off-peak; can be much longer in peak traffic (5-9 PM).
- Traffic is the dominant constraint. For time-critical events (stroke = "FAST" symptoms, MI = chest pain radiating), consider private transport directly to hospital if 108 ETA is high.
- Hospitals (24/7 emergency): Manipal (HAL Road / Whitefield / Hebbal / Jayanagar), Apollo (Jayanagar / Bannerghatta / Sheshadripuram), Fortis (BG Road / Cunningham), Narayana Health City (Bommasandra — major cardiac), Victoria Hospital (govt, Kalasipalyam — free, busy), Bowring (govt, central). NIMHANS is the national-level mental health referral.
- **Monsoon (Jun-Sep)** flooding: low-lying ORR pockets, Silk Board, Bellandur often submerge. Plan alternate route. Do NOT drive through flowing water deeper than 6 inches.
- **Snake bite:** Bangalore peripheries (esp. ORR, Whitefield, Sarjapur) have monsoon snake encounters. Don't apply tourniquet, don't cut, don't suck venom. Immobilize limb, mark fang site time, get to a hospital with antivenom (Manipal, Apollo, govt hospitals stock it).
- **Tech-park emergencies:** Most large tech parks (Manyata, EGL, ITPL, Whitefield SEZs) have on-site medical rooms with AEDs and basic ambulance tie-ups; use them before public 108 if response time matters.
- **Bangalore Police "Suraksha" app** allows panic-button + GPS to nearest patrol car.
