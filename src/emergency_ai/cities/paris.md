## Emergency numbers

- Primary (police / fire / ambulance): **112**
- Samu Medical: **15**
- Police: **17**
- Fire Pompiers: **18**
- Eu Emergency: **112**
- Suicide Prevention: **3114**
- Poison Control: **01-40-05-48-48**

## Local laws relevant in emergencies

- **Duty to Assist a Person in Danger** (Code Pénal Art. 223-6): French law imposes a positive legal obligation on every person to assist someone in danger, provided they can do so without risk to themselves. Failure to assist is a criminal offence punishable by up to 5 years imprisonment and €75,000 fine.
- **Good Samaritan Liability Protection** (Code de la Santé Publique Art. L1110-5-3): A person who provides emergency assistance in good faith cannot be held civilly or criminally liable for harm caused by that assistance, provided they acted reasonably.

## Practical notes

- France has three emergency lines: 15 (SAMU — medical), 17 (police), 18 (pompiers/fire). 112 reaches all three and is the recommended single number.
- SAMU (15) dispatches a regulation médecin if needed; they may triage by phone and send the appropriate response level.
- Paris Métro emergency: press the red alarm button on platforms. Do not use it between stations.
- French law requires assisting a person in danger (non-assistance à personne en danger) — bystanders have a legal duty to call for help at minimum.
- Heat waves (July-August): France implemented Plan Canicule after 2003. Elderly in top-floor apartments without AC are highest risk. Call 15 for heat-related emergencies.
- Paris attacks protocol: Run-Hide-Tell (Protéger-Alerter-Secourir). Follow police instructions; stay off streets.
