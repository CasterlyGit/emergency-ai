## Emergency numbers

- All-emergency single number: **112** (works mobile + landline)
- Police: **100**
- Fire: **101**
- Ambulance: **102** (govt) or **108** (Maharashtra integrated service)
- Women's Helpline: **1091**
- Child Helpline: **1098**
- Disaster Management (Mumbai): **1916** (BMC) / **022-22694725**

## Local laws relevant in emergencies

- **Good Samaritan Law (India, Supreme Court 2016 + MoRTH Guidelines 2020):** Anyone who helps a road accident victim is protected from civil/criminal liability and cannot be detained at the hospital or police station for questioning beyond a single statement. Identity disclosure is optional.
- **Hospital duty (Pt. Parmanand Katara v. Union of India, 1989):** Every doctor, government or private, has a constitutional duty to provide immediate emergency care, regardless of medico-legal formalities. You can demand this — they cannot refuse stating "MLC formalities."
- **Domestic Violence Act 2005:** Protection officers can be reached via 1091 or directly at the BMC ward office. Magistrate-issued protection orders are enforceable.
- **Mental Healthcare Act 2017:** Decriminalized attempted suicide. A person attempting suicide should be presumed to be under severe stress and given care, not detained.

## Practical notes

- **108 ambulance** is the most reliable in Mumbai for true emergencies — GPS-tracked, free, govt-run. 102 also free but typically slower. Private ambulances charge but may be faster in traffic.
- Traffic: monsoon (Jun-Sep) can paralyze city movement. For a heart attack or stroke during peak traffic / flooding, the fastest route may be **a private vehicle directly to the hospital** rather than waiting for ambulance access.
- Hospitals: Major emergency-capable: KEM (Parel), Sion Hospital, JJ Hospital, Nair, Cooper, Lilavati (Bandra), Hinduja (Mahim), Kokilaben (Andheri). KEM/Sion/JJ are govt and free.
- **Local trains:** in a medical emergency, pull the alarm chain (Rs.1000 fine but waived for genuine emergency). At stations, the Station Master's office has first-aid and direct hospital lines.
- Mumbai Police App and "Mumbai 112" mobile app allow GPS-located emergency reporting — useful when you can't speak.
- **Drug overdose:** No specific drug Good Samaritan law. Calling 108 is usually safe; police involvement is at the hospital's MLC discretion.
