## Emergency numbers

- Primary (police / fire / ambulance): **911**
- Poison Control: **1-800-222-1222**
- Crisis Lifeline: **988**
- Copa Non Emergency: **312-744-5000**
- Non Emergency: **311**

## Local laws relevant in emergencies

- **Illinois Good Samaritan Act** (745 ILCS 49): Any person who in good faith provides emergency care without fee is immune from civil liability unless the act constitutes willful or wanton misconduct.
- **Illinois Drug Overdose Prevention Act** (20 ILCS 301/5-501.5): A person who calls 911 in good faith to report a drug overdose emergency is granted limited immunity from arrest and prosecution for small amounts of controlled substances.

## Practical notes

- CFD (Chicago Fire Department) handles medical emergencies. IDPH licenses all paramedics who respond via 911.
- Extreme cold (wind chill below -15°F): Chicago issues Warming Center activations. Call 311 for nearest warming center; hypothermia is life-threatening within minutes in severe wind chill.
- Chicago 'L' emergency: use the intercom button on each rail car. Do not pull the brake unless there is an immediate life-threatening reason.
- Violence and trauma: Chicago has active trauma networks — Advocate Christ, Northwestern, Stroger — all with Level 1 status.
- Heat emergencies (July-August): call 311 for cooling center locations. The 1995 heat wave killed 739 — take heat illness seriously.
